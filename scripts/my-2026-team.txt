STRATEGY NOTE
-------------
2026 rules heavily reward youth. With +4 points to the BEST YOUNG
FINISHER (not jersey holder) every stage, a top-5 youth-eligible rider
can earn 80+ bonus points across the race. The extended reserve window
(stage 10 vs. 6) also means reserves who enter early have real value —
so a slightly riskier main is acceptable if the reserve is strong.

The team is built around two unassailable GC anchors, a deep youth-bonus
bench, and enough sprinter/all-rounder coverage to score on flat days.


MAINS (15)
----------

1.  TADEJ POGAČAR (UAE Team Emirates)
    2025 TdF: 276 pts | 4 stage wins
    The pick you cannot not make. Won 2024 and 2025; if he finishes he
    scores 60–70 pts from GC alone before a single stage win. No injury
    concerns. Scores youth bonus on mountain stages — born 1998, just
    inside U26 for 2026.

2.  JONAS VINGEGAARD (Visma-Lease a Bike)
    2025 TdF: 198 pts
    Two-time winner. Even when not winning he hauls in GC points.
    Visma builds the race around him; he almost never abandons. The
    floor on this pick is extremely high.

3.  REMCO EVENEPOEL (Soudal Quick-Step)
    2025 TdF: 36 pts (limited by Vuelta focus)
    When Evenepoel targets the Tour he finishes podium (3rd in 2024).
    Born 2000 — U26 in 2026 = full youth bonus eligibility. A stage
    on the TT alone is worth 8–10 pts + youth bonus. High upside.

4.  PRIMOŽ ROGLIČ (Red Bull-Bora-Hansgrohe)
    2025 TdF: 45 pts
    Veteran GC who reliably finishes top-5 and hunts stages. The Bora
    team is built for two leaders (Roglič + Lipowitz), doubling our
    coverage on that roster.

5.  FLORIAN LIPOWITZ (Red Bull-Bora-Hansgrohe)
    2025 TdF: 108 pts | Born 2001 — youth eligible
    Breakout 2025 result. Strong enough to contest GC (top-5 threat)
    AND young enough to earn the youth bonus most mountain stages.
    Best single pick for combined GC + youth value.

6.  OSCAR ONLEY (dsm-firmenich PostNL)
    2025 TdF: 105 pts | Born 2002 — youth eligible
    Another 2025 revelation. Pure climber who can stay with the GC
    group on summit finishes. Youth bonus on every hard mountain stage;
    could sweep the bonus on days Lipowitz is off the front.

7.  BEN HEALY (EF Education-EasyPost)
    2025 TdF: 72 pts | Born 2000 — youth eligible
    The most dangerous breakaway rider in the peloton. Attacks from
    distance on hard stages. Youth bonus candidate when he gets clear.
    EF's entire race plan is "send Healy" — reliable stage-hunting.

8.  JONATHAN MILAN (Lidl-Trek)
    2025 TdF: 70 pts
    Best pure sprinter in the race. Wins flat stages that no other
    pick on this team targets. GC riders score 0 on sprint finishes;
    Milan fills that gap every flat stage.

9.  FELIX GALL (Decathlon AG2R La Mondiale)
    2025 TdF: 68 pts
    Consistently top-10 in the mountains. Low crash risk (climber style,
    rarely in sprint chaos), strong enough to stay in the GC group.
    Reliable 40–70 pts baseline.

10. THYMEN ARENSMAN (INEOS Grenadiers)
    2025 TdF: 55 pts | Born 1999 — youth eligible (just inside U26)
    GC support rider who goes for stages when teammates don't need him.
    INEOS always has a TdF plan; Arensman is their best young option.

11. WOUT VAN AERT (Visma-Lease a Bike)
    2025 TdF: 53 pts
    All-rounder who scores in time trials, cobble stages, Pyrénées
    finishes, and bunch sprints. Impossible to predict which stages he
    wins, which is part of the value — diversified scoring across
    every stage type.

12. KEVIN VAUQUELIN (Arkéa-B&B Hotels)
    2025 TdF: 51 pts | Born 2000 — youth eligible
    French climber who thrives under Tour pressure (home crowds push
    him). Actively chases youth-bonus stages when out of GC contention.
    Good value relative to roster cost.

13. TOBIAS JOHANNESSEN (Uno-X Mobility)
    2025 TdF: 48 pts | Born 2002 — youth eligible
    Norwegian climber who came of age in 2025. Pure mountain stage
    hunter; when the GC leaders neutralise each other he attacks.
    Youth bonus upside on days Lipowitz/Onley go for GC.

14. ARNAUD DE LIE (Lotto Dstny)
    2025 TdF: 45 pts | Born 2002 — youth eligible
    The youth bonus on flat stages. When the bunch sprint happens,
    De Lie is young enough to sweep the youth classification finish;
    Milan wins the stage but De Lie gets +4 youth bonus if he outsprints
    other U26 riders. Validated: in 2025 he was best youth finisher on
    multiple sprint stages (Healy held the jersey but De Lie took the
    per-stage award).

15. MATTEO JORGENSON (Visma-Lease a Bike)
    2025 TdF: 33 pts | Born 1999 — youth eligible
    Improving GC trajectory (US champion, strong TT). Visma insurance:
    if Vingegaard struggles, Jorgenson inherits the GC role and keeps
    scoring. Low downside — 33p floor, GC podium ceiling.


RESERVES (3)
------------

R1. MATHIEU VAN DER POEL (Alpecin-Deceuninck)
    2025 TdF: 51 pts
    Rationale: MvdP targets the cobbled stages and opening week, then
    often abandons before the mountains. With reserve_lock=10, if he
    drops out in stages 1–10 we automatically sub in R2 and still get
    full mountain coverage. Use him for opening-week stage wins;
    treat abandonment as a feature not a bug.

R2. ROMAIN GRÉGOIRE (Groupama-FDJ)
    2025 TdF: 26 pts | Born 2002 — youth eligible
    Young French climber with stage-winning ability. Enters as R1's
    replacement if MvdP DNFs early. Young enough to continue earning
    youth bonuses through the mountains.

R3. CARLOS RODRÍGUEZ (INEOS Grenadiers)
    Reserve for a GC rider loss. If Roglič, Evenepoel, or a top-5 main
    drops before stage 10, Rodríguez inherits their GC role. Strong
    enough for top-10 GC, consistent mountain scorer (finished 4th TdF
    2023). Low volatility insurance for the back half of the race.
